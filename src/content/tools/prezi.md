longDescription: "Prezi pioneered the non-linear presentation format, replacing traditional slide decks with a zooming canvas where ideas are arranged spatially and navigated through a path of zoom-in and zoom-out transitions. This approach gives presenters more flexibility to jump between topics, respond to audience questions by navigating to relevant sections, and create a sense of visual storytelling that static slides struggle to replicate.\n\nThe platform has evolved significantly from its early days. Prezi now includes AI-powered design features that help users create presentations faster — generating layouts, suggesting visual structures, and auto-formatting content as you add it. The editor supports text, images, video, charts, and custom shapes arranged on an infinite canvas, giving users creative freedom that traditional slide tools don't offer.\n\nPrezi Video is a standout feature that overlays your presentation content alongside your webcam feed during video calls. Instead of sharing your screen and disappearing behind slides, your content appears next to you — making remote presentations feel more personal and engaging. This works with Zoom, Teams, Google Meet, and Webex.\n\nThe platform also offers Prezi Design for creating interactive reports, infographics, and visual documents that go beyond presentations. For teams, Prezi includes collaboration features, shared brand kits, and analytics showing how viewers engage with shared presentations. While the zooming format isn't for everyone — some audiences find it disorienting — for the right use cases, Prezi creates a presentation experience that no traditional tool can match."
